The Forces of the Dark Lord of Mordor

The forces of darkness seek for total dominion over all life. Sauron, Second Dark Lord to terrorise Middle Earth, created the One Ring to rule them all for this very purpose. Not just Trolls and Orcs but scores of Evil Men make up Saurons legions and all will answer his call. Play as the evil forces of the world and seek to destroy and dominate the forces of Good. the

Mordor

Gondor, the Southern Kingdom of the Númenóreans – founded in the Second Age by Elendil, and ruled by his sons Isildur and Anárion. After the death of Isildur at the Disaster of the Gladden Fields, Gondor fell on hard times. With the return of the Dark Lord of Mordor, and Gondor so close to darkness can the enemy be pushed back and finally defeated.

Isengard

The Valley of Dale lies directly south of the Lonely Mountain and overlooks the Long Lake. Destroyed by the Dragon Smaug on his ruinous path to Erebor the survivors fled South to live with the lake-men of Esgaroth. In 2941 Bard the Bowman, heir to the throne of Dale, shot and killed the dragon, Smaug and allied with dwarves of the Iron Hills and Elves from the Woodland Realm defeated the armies of Orcs and Wargs at the battle of the Five Armies.

Orcs of the Misty Mountains

The Kingdom of Rohan was established in the Third Age, when the cavalry forces of the Kingdom of Rhovanion came to Gondors aid in destroying an army Easterlings and forced them to flee beyond Greenwood the Great. These Northmen were the Horse-People who called themselves Eotheod but by the elves they were known as the Rohirrim (people of the horse). In return for there aid, Gondor bestowed upon them the lands between the Rivers Isen and Anduin, the land that came to be known as Rohan.

Orcs of Gundabad

The Lands between the Misty Mountains and the Blue Mountains are named Eriador. In the year 3320 of the Second Age, Elendil established the Northern Kingdom of Numenor - Arnor. After the battle of the Last Alliance and the death of Elendil and his son Anarion, this land fell into decline due to civil strife and later the war with Angmar and the Witch-king. In 1974 the Witch-King destroyed the capital at Fornost and with it the last of the Dunedain in the North. Only a part of the Dunedain led by Aradart descendent of Isildur survived to live in hiding. His descendents still live among the peaceful folk of Eriador, ranging the lands and keeping its people safe from evil, awaiting the day they might return to greatness.

Harad

Doriath, the hidden kingdom, was the greatest of the Sindarin realms in Beleriand, but during the wars it was destroyed. Many of those who survived fled east past the towering peaks of the Hithaeglir into the lands where the Tawarwait, Silvan Elves, long sundered from the elves of Beleriand, dwelled. Being a rustic people lesser in wisdom, the silvans took the Sindar as their lords. During the Last Alliance of Men and Elves these lords Amdir and Oropher were slain along with most of their great host. Thranduil, son of Oropher led his people North his heart full of disquiet and dissillusionment and established the Woodland Realm. There he delved a great underground fortress. The lord and lady of the Wood of Lothlorien took up guardianship of that realm. There the hardy but less well equipped Eastern elves stand as a fortress against the encroaching darkness. The Silvan Elves are unmatched in their skill with a bow and their hatred of Sauron runs deep.

Rhun

After the destruction of Beleriand the Noldor, and Sindar who followed them, passed to Lindon and Eregion and established Mithlond, the Grey Havens. Sauron deceived the elves of Eregion into the creation of the Rings of Power. After his creation of the One ring the elves perceived his malice and hid the Three Elven Rings. In return Sauron destroyed Eregion and slew Celebrimbor the leader of the Elves in this region. Survivors led by Elrond Halfelven established Imladris or Rivendell in the foothills of the Misty Mountain. With the help of the Men of Numenor in the Last Alliance Sauron was defeated, although the power of the Noldor was greatly diminished. Today the realms of Mithlond and Imladris have but a whisper of their old glory and longer have the strength to openly march against the resurgent power of Mordor. The valor of the elves has not diminished however and by their vigilance the land East of the Misty Mountains are kept safe.
